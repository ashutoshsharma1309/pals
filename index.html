05 Frequently Asked

Quick answers. Faster builds.

01 Who can participate?
All BE, ME, MTech, and MCA students from every branch of BMSIT are eligible to participate. Bring curiosity — the rest is provided.
02 Is prior experience required?
No. The workshop preceding the hackathon is built precisely so first-timers can sprint alongside seasoned hackers.
03 Will certificates be provided?
Yes. Every participant receives an official certificate from PALs Club, BMSIT — winners get something a little shinier.
04 Is this a team event?
Yes. Teams are formed on-site to encourage cross-branch collaboration and to prevent anyone from coding alone at 3 AM.
05 Will food be provided?
Refreshments and meals are provided during workshop and build hours. Caffeine, importantly, is non-negotiable.
06 What should participants bring?
Laptop, charger, college ID, any hardware components your idea needs, and a willingness to ship something real.
07 Is there a registration fee?
No. Participation is completely free for all eligible BMSIT students.
08 Can I bring my own idea, or are problem statements provided?
Both. We share a set of curated problem statements across hardware and software tracks, but original ideas aligned with the tracks are equally welcome.
09 Will mentors be available during the hackathon?
Yes. Faculty and industry mentors circulate throughout the build sprint to unstick teams on hardware, software, and pitch direction.
10 How will projects be judged?
Projects are judged on innovation, technical execution, real-world impact, and the quality of the final demo presented to the panel.
11 How big can a team be?
Team sizes will be announced along with the final schedule. Cross-branch and cross-year teams are encouraged.
12 Will Wi-Fi and power be available at the venue?
Yes — campus Wi-Fi and dedicated power points will be available throughout the workshop and the 8-hour build sprint.
